Why is a Resume Important?

Your resume is the first impression a recruiter has of you. A professional resume clearly presents your education, technical skills, projects, internships, certifications, achievements, and career objectives in a simple and attractive format. A strong resume increases your chances of getting shortlisted for interviews.

Essential Resume Sections

👤

Personal Information

Include your full name, professional email, phone number, LinkedIn profile, GitHub profile and location.

🎓

Education

Mention your degree, college, CGPA, graduation year and relevant coursework.

💻

Technical Skills

List HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, SQL, Git, GitHub and other technologies.

📂

Projects

Explain your projects, technologies used, features developed and your contributions.

🏢

Internship

Mention your internship experience, testing work, responsibilities, and achievements.

🏆

Certifications

Include certifications from FreeCodeCamp, Coursera, Cisco, Microsoft, NPTEL or Udemy.

Resume Writing Tips

✔

Keep it Short

A fresher's resume should ideally be one page. Recruiters spend only a few seconds reviewing each resume, so keep it concise and easy to read.

💻

Highlight Skills

Mention your programming languages, tools, frameworks, databases, and other technical skills that match the job description.

📂

Show Your Projects

Include your best academic or personal projects with technologies used, features implemented, and the results achieved.

🏢

Add Internship Experience

Describe your internship responsibilities, testing activities, development tasks, and accomplishments in clear bullet points.

ATS Friendly Resume Tips

  • ✔ Use simple fonts like Poppins, Arial, or Calibri.
  • ✔ Keep headings clear and consistent.
  • ✔ Include keywords from the job description.
  • ✔ Save your resume as a PDF unless instructed otherwise.
  • ✔ Avoid excessive graphics and complicated tables.
  • ✔ Use action words such as Developed, Tested, Designed, Created, and Implemented.
  • ✔ Add measurable achievements whenever possible.
  • ✔ Proofread carefully for spelling and grammar.

Common Resume Mistakes

❌

Grammar Errors

Always proofread your resume before submitting it.

📄

Long Resume

Avoid adding unnecessary information that makes your resume too lengthy.

🎨

Poor Formatting

Maintain consistent spacing, fonts, and headings throughout your resume.

🚫

Irrelevant Details

Include only information that is relevant to the role you're applying for.
